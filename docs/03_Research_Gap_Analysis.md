# Research Gap Analysis

Current structural methodologies in computational psychological diagnostics reveal severe systemic limitations:

* **Inflexibility & Static Models**: Most frameworks rely on frozen model weights (non-adaptive), completely lacking longitudinal data validation paradigms. They do not respond to changing user behaviors over time.
* **Data Silos**: Diagnostic tools generally examine isolated data channels (only public text OR private surveys). Merging these differences in data types is rare, which weakens effectiveness.
* **Lack of Transparency (Opaque Models)**: Traditional deep neural models (Black-box) cause low field adoption rates. Users need to trust the conclusions regarding their mental state, which requires Explainable AI (XAI).
* **Absence of Preventative Triggers**: Systems typically activate reactively after an acute emotional scenario occurs instead of providing proactive prevention strategies (e.g., early journaling or meditation prompts).
* **Cultural & Linguistic Bias**: Most emotion detection models work predominantly on English data, ignoring regional languages and cultural non-verbal expressions.
